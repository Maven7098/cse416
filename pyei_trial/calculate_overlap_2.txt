✦ To calculate the overlap between the two distributions shown in the ei_2by2.plot_kde()
  graph (which represents the posterior distributions of voting preferences for the
  selected demographic group and its complement), you can use the posterior samples
  stored in the TwoByTwoEI object.

  The samples are available in the sampled_voting_prefs dictionary of your ei_2by2
  instance. You can calculate the Overlapping Coefficient (OVL)—the area under the
  minimum of the two density functions—using either a histogram-based approach or by
  fitting Kernel Density Estimators (KDE) and integrating their intersection.

  Recommended Implementation

  You can add the following code to presidential_ei.py after the ei_2by2.fit() call:

    1 from scipy.stats import gaussian_kde
    2
    3 # 1. Extract the samples for the two groups
    4 # Group 0 is your selected demographic (e.g., Black)
    5 # Group 1 is the complement (e.g., Non-Black)
    6 samples_group = ei_2by2.sampled_voting_prefs[0]
    7 samples_complement = ei_2by2.sampled_voting_prefs[1]
    8
    9 # 2. Estimate the KDE for both sets of samples
   10 kde_group = gaussian_kde(samples_group)
   11 kde_complement = gaussian_kde(samples_complement)
   12
   13 # 3. Calculate the intersection over the [0, 1] range
   14 x = np.linspace(0, 1, 1000)
   15 y_group = kde_group(x)
   16 y_complement = kde_complement(x)
   17
   18 # The overlap is the area under the minimum of the two curves
   19 overlap = np.trapz(np.minimum(y_group, y_complement), x)
   20
   21 print(f"Overlap between {demographic_group_name} and Non-{demographic_group_name}:
      {overlap:.4f}")
   22
   23 # Optional: Save the overlap value to your output file
   24 with open(f"{sys.argv[4]}.txt", "a") as f:
   25     f.write(f"Distribution Overlap: {overlap:.4f}\n")

  Explanation
   - ei_2by2.sampled_voting_prefs[0]: Contains the MCMC samples for the voting preference
     ($b_1$) of the demographic group you specified (e.g., Black support for Harris).
   - ei_2by2.sampled_voting_prefs[1]: Contains the samples for the complement group
     ($b_2$).
   - gaussian_kde: Fits a smooth curve to these samples, matching the lines seen in the
     plot_kde() graph.
   - np.minimum & np.trapz: Find the intersection of these two curves and integrate to
     find the total area of overlap. An overlap of 1.0 means the distributions are
     identical, while 0.0 means they are completely separate.

  Simplified Histogram Version
  If you prefer a faster calculation without fitting KDEs, you can use numpy.histogram:

   1 bins = 1000
   2 h1, _ = np.histogram(samples_group, bins=bins, range=(0, 1), density=True)
   3 h2, _ = np.histogram(samples_complement, bins=bins, range=(0, 1), density=True)
   4 overlap = np.sum(np.minimum(h1, h2)) / bins

  This will give you a very close approximation to the visual overlap in the graph.

✦ I have updated presidential_ei.py to calculate the Overlapping Coefficient (OVL)
  between the two distributions shown in the KDE plot and store it in two separate files.

  Changes Made:
   1. Imports: Added json and scipy.stats.gaussian_kde to the script.
   2. Calculation: Added logic to extract the posterior samples for both groups, estimate
      their probability densities using Kernel Density Estimation (KDE), and integrate
      the intersection using the trapezoidal rule.
   3. Compatibility: Used np.trapezoid to ensure compatibility with NumPy 2.x (which
      removed np.trapz).
   4. Storage: 
       * Text File: The overlap value is appended to the existing .txt summary file.
       * JSON File: A new file named {output_prefix}-Overlap.json is created, containing
         the overlap value (e.g., {"overlap": 0.0421}).

  How to use:
  When you run the script, it will now automatically generate the overlap file along with
  your plots:

   1 ./pyei_venv/bin/python3 presidential_ei.py GA-Precinct-EI.csv Black Harris
     ga/GA-Polarization-Black-Harris
  This will produce ga/GA-Polarization-Black-Harris-Overlap.json and add an "Overlap in
  KDE distributions" line to ga/GA-Polarization-Black-Harris.txt.

  An overlap of 1.0 indicates identical distributions, while 0.0 indicates no overlap at
  all (common when groups have vastly different voting preferences).
